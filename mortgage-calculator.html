Definitions

Home Value ($): The total value of the home you are purchasing. This is the purchase price of the property.

Down Payment (%): The percentage of the home value you will pay upfront. This reduces the loan amount you need to borrow.

Loan Term (years): The number of years over which you will repay the loan. Common terms are 15 or 30 years.

Interest Rate (%): The annual interest rate on the loan. This is the cost of borrowing the money, expressed as a percentage.

Start Month: The month when the loan repayment will start. This helps to calculate the payment schedule accurately.

Start Year: The year when the loan repayment will start. This is used in conjunction with the start month.

Double Payments: Option to double your monthly payments. This can help pay off the loan faster and reduce interest costs.

Balloon Payment ($): A large payment made at a specified period during the loan term. This is usually a lump sum paid at the end of the loan term.

Balloon Payment Period (months): The period (in months) when the balloon payment will be made. For example, a balloon payment at 60 months means a large payment at the end of 5 years.
